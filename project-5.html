Hand Gesture Controlled Audio Effects Processor

Live music performance lack a handsfree solution for applying audio effects to music. I build a hand gesture controlled audio effects processor which detects hand gestures and applies the corresponding audio effects

Demo

Project Overview

This project proposes a gesture-controlled audio effects processor to address accessibility and practicality concerns in audio production and live music settings. By leveraging camera-based gesture detection, users can manipulate audio effects in real-time without the need for traditional physical interfaces like buttons or knobs. This innovative approach offers a dynamic and expressive means of controlling audio effects, particularly beneficial for individuals with disabilities or those navigating complex production environments. With the ability to adjust various effect parameters through intuitive hand gestures, the device provides a streamlined alternative to conventional DJ controllers or hardware processors, enhancing both accessibility and versatility in audio manipulation.

High Level Architecture

The design was modular in the sense each subsystem can be made on its own and then intergrated together using data busses.

Hand Gesture Detection

To do hand gesture detected a neural net was needed to convert the image of hands to their joints. Google's mediapipe library has a pre trained model which gives information on the following joints

Once I have joint information, I can take two points on the hand (the tip and the joint 2 under it) and check which one is closer to the top of the screen. Based on that information, I can determine if a finger is up or down. With information on which fingers are up or down, I was able to detect a hand gesture
